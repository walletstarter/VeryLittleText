Midnight Signal

2024-01-02

The town’s radio tower blinked a lonely red pulse against the midnight clouds. Jessa watched from her attic room, counting the rhythm like breaths. She tuned a rusted handheld radio, hoping for a secret song. Only static greeted her, yet she imagined voices riding the hiss, travelers announcing their arrival. The pulse continued, a heartbeat for the sleeping city. Jessa tapped her own answer on the windowpane, three soft beats at a time. Somewhere between the pulse and the static, she believed someone answered back.
