Between Pages

2024-01-06

A tattered paperback lived on the cafe counter, free for any guest to borrow. Nadia opened it midway and found a dried leaf pressed flat, veins forming a map. She traced the paths with her finger, imagining cities at each intersection. The book's previous reader had underlined a sentence: 'Every map is a promise.' She closed the cover, sliding the leaf into her notebook to keep the promise for later. That evening, she used it as a bookmark in a new story, and the map began to grow.
