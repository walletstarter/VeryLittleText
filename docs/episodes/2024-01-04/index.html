Library of Hums

2024-01-04

The old library had lost its electricity, yet patrons still came. Candles lined the aisles, lending each shelf a soft halo. Marta volunteered to guide visitors with a lantern and a hum. She could not sing, but the low tune kept nerves steady and pages turning. Readers hummed along, creating a quiet choir among biographies and poetry. When power returned with a flicker, the lights felt too loud. The patrons lingered in the afterglow, reluctant to surrender the chorus that had held the silence together.
