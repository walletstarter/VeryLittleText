River Learning

2024-01-03

The river behind the school carried more than water. During lunch, Leo sat with his sandwich and watched driftwood negotiate the bends. Each snag became a small lesson in patience. A goose paddled by, eyeing him as if grading his silence. When the bell rang, he stayed, letting the current decide the pace of afternoon. He imagined all the notebooks the river might have swallowed, pages dissolving into the current, ideas shared with fish. Leo returned to class late, but carrying a new fluid handwriting in his mind.
